Find Your FAVORITE Restaurant in NYC!

By: Xuesen Zhao | Tianshu Liu | Xicheng Xie | Jiayi Shi | Lincole Jiang

Background

According to the data from Statista, there had been over twenty-seven thousand restaurants in New York City by the end of 2018. Although more than a thousand restaurants were shut down or temporarily closed due to the COVID-19 pandemic, some restaurants quickly adapted to offering deliveries, takeout and outdoor dining. By mid May, New York City reopened indoor dining at full capacity. Meanwhile, as public schools, colleges and increasingly more companies reverted back to in-person operations, or at least a hybrid mode, the needs for dining start to grow rapidly once again.

While it is glad to see restaurants reopening and even new ones opening, it is crucial to keep up with rigorous inspection on food storage and processing, dining environment, and kitchen management to ensure public health safety. Such a year-long stagnancy on the restaurant industry also means potential needs for old facility check-ups, new employee training for appropriate food handling, proper waste disposal, cold-chain management, and kitchen sanitation. Hence, we are interested in not only the customers’ review of the restaurants in NYC, but also the performance of these restaurant during inspection programs held by the NYC Health Department.

Datasets

With this in mind, we cited NYC restaurant inspection data that is made available to the public on Opendata This dataset contains detailed information on restaurant and college cafeteria inspection results, up to three years prior to the most recent inspection. The original dataset has over 230 thousand rows and 27 columns by December 1st, 2022. Some examples of the variables include: inspection score and corresponding letter grade, violation description, date, types of cuisine, and the borough in which the restaurant is located in, etc.

After identifying the first 6053 distinct restaurant with latest inspection records, we extracted their name and location information to generate Yelp searching URLs, and used the corresponding CSS tags to pull the restaurant name, review scores, price, number of reviews, and location for each restaurant.

Objectives & Main Questions

  • What is the geographical distibution of restaurants with respect to price, cuisine type, or other characteristic of the restaurant?

  • Is there any pattern related to inspection information and cuisine types across different boroughs and price levels?

  • Can we set up an interactive recommender to provide appropriate searching results as well as inspection records?
